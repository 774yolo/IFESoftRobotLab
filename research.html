Research

Soft Robot Sensing

Compliant strain sensors are crucial for soft robots to realize real-time sensing of various environmental stimuli as feedback to enhance robot perception and autonomy. However, the deformable nature of robot bodies and their dynamic actuation behaviors pose an urgent and challenging task in achieving predictive sensor manufacturing and long-term robustness for practical robot applications. Here, we focus on the development of accurate sensor modeling tools and well-controlled sensor dynamics.

Ref:
Nature Communications, 2024, 15, 1636
Nature Machine Intelligence, 2022, 4, 84

Soft Robot Communicating

Tight integration of advanced wireless communication techniques enables more effective cooperation among soft robots, posing an urgent need for high‑performance flexible antennas. However, state‑of‑the‑art flexible antennas usually show limited stretchability, which hinders their application on highly elastic soft robot bodies. Here, we aim to develop mechanically resilient nanomaterials‑based antennas for soft robots.

Ref: Science Robotics, 2019, 4, eaax7020

Soft Robot Computing

Machine intelligence is significant for soft robots to respond smartly to the external world. To achieve embodied intelligence, fusing machine learning algorithms and edge computing techniques shows high potential. However, it remains challenging to process high‑throughput and noisy data on edge chips with compact models in a power‑efficient fashion.

Ref: Nature Communications, 2022, 13, 5311
